Why People Choose North Las Vegas

North Las Vegas has emerged as one of the fastest-growing cities in Nevada, attracting residents from across the country with its combination of affordability, opportunity, and quality of life. Several factors make the area particularly attractive to relocating families and individuals.

Cost of Living Advantages

Nevada's lack of state income tax provides immediate financial benefits for most relocating workers. Combined with housing costs that are below the national average for comparable quality, North Las Vegas offers significant cost of living advantages compared to many major metropolitan areas, particularly those in California, the Northeast, and other high-cost regions.

The Waterfall neighborhood exemplifies this value proposition. Homes with resort-style amenities, gated security, and quality construction are available at price points that would be impossible in many other desirable communities across the country. For families relocating from high-cost areas, the purchasing power difference can be dramatic.

Understanding the Las Vegas Valley

The Las Vegas Valley encompasses several cities and communities, each with its own character and advantages. Understanding the geography helps newcomers appreciate Waterfall's location and accessibility.

North Las Vegas is located north of the city of Las Vegas and offers a more suburban, family-oriented environment while maintaining easy access to employment centers, entertainment, and amenities throughout the valley. The 215 Beltway provides convenient connections to all parts of the metropolitan area, making commutes manageable regardless of where you work.

Climate and Weather

The desert climate takes some adjustment for newcomers from other regions. Summers are hot, with temperatures regularly exceeding 100°F from June through September. However, the low humidity makes the heat more tolerable than comparable temperatures in humid climates. Winters are mild, with daytime temperatures typically in the 50s and 60s and occasional nights near freezing.

The abundance of sunshine—over 300 sunny days per year—is one of the area's greatest attractions. Outdoor activities are possible year-round, and the clear skies and mountain views create a beautiful living environment. Pools and outdoor amenities like those in Waterfall are usable for much of the year.

What to Know Before You Move

Successful relocation requires understanding both the practical aspects of moving and the characteristics of your new community. Here's essential information for those considering a move to Waterfall.

Schools

Waterfall is served by Clark County School District with zoned schools at all levels. Charter and private options are also available throughout the valley.

Healthcare

Multiple hospitals and medical facilities serve North Las Vegas, including VA facilities for veterans. Specialists are available throughout the valley.

Airport Access

Harry Reid International Airport is approximately 20 minutes from Waterfall, providing convenient travel connections nationwide.

Shopping & Dining

Extensive retail and dining options nearby, from everyday shopping to world-class restaurants on the Las Vegas Strip.

Recreation

Outdoor recreation abounds with Red Rock Canyon, Lake Mead, Mt. Charleston, and Valley of Fire all within easy driving distance.

Entertainment

World-class entertainment, dining, and nightlife on the Las Vegas Strip, plus professional sports teams and cultural attractions.

Employment and Economy

The Las Vegas Valley economy has diversified significantly beyond gaming and tourism, though these industries remain important employers. Healthcare, technology, logistics, and professional services have grown substantially, providing diverse employment opportunities for relocating professionals.

Major employers in the area include gaming and hospitality companies, healthcare systems like Sunrise Health and Dignity Health, technology companies, and government agencies. The growth of remote work has also made the area attractive to professionals who can work from anywhere while enjoying Nevada's tax advantages and lifestyle benefits.

Starting a Business

Nevada's business-friendly environment attracts entrepreneurs and small business owners. The state offers favorable tax treatment, streamlined business formation processes, and a growing economy that supports new ventures. Many relocating professionals have successfully established businesses in the Las Vegas Valley.

Practical Relocation Considerations

Moving to a new state involves numerous practical considerations beyond finding a home. Here are key items to address as you plan your relocation:

  • Driver's License: Nevada requires new residents to obtain a Nevada driver's license within 30 days
  • Vehicle Registration: Vehicles must be registered in Nevada within 30 days of establishing residency
  • Voter Registration: Register to vote in Nevada to participate in local and state elections
  • Utilities: Establish accounts with NV Energy (electric), Southwest Gas, and Las Vegas Valley Water District
  • Healthcare: Find new healthcare providers and transfer medical records
  • Schools: Register children with Clark County School District or chosen private/charter schools
